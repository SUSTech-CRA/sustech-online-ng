## 讲座简介
Cells must constantly sense and adapt to fluctuations in energy and nutrient availability to maintain homeostasis and ensure survival. Our research explores two interconnected layers of this adaptive network: the mitochondrial stress response and nutrient sensing mechanisms. We will discuss how cells monitor mitochondrial function and activate stress signaling pathways to restore proteostasis and metabolic balance under energetic stress, highlighting how these pathways contribute to organismal resilience and aging. We will also present our recent discoveries in nutrient sensing, focusing on how cells detect nutrient levels through the mechanistic target of rapamycin complex 1 (mTORC1) pathway, and how this process links nutrient availability to growth and metabolism. Together, these findings provide an integrated view of how cells sense and respond to energy and nutrient stress, coordinating metabolic adaptation from the organelle to the organismal level.
